Mosaic&trade;</h3>\t\t\n\t\t<p>NCSA\'s Mosaic&#153; wasn\'t the first Web browser. But it was the first to make a major splash. In November 1993, <a href="/enabling/mosaic/versions">Mosaic v 1.0</a> broke away from the small pack of existing browsers by including features&mdash;like icons, bookmarks, a more attractive interface, and pictures&mdash;that made the software easy to use and appealing to "non-geeks."</p>\n\n<p>NCSA offered Mosaic free <a href="ftp://ftp.ncsa.uiuc.edu/Mosaic/">from its website</a>, and soon more than 5,000 copies were being downloaded each month; the center was receiving hundreds of thousands of email inquiries a week, and <a href="/assets/img/enabling/mosaic/impact.gif">Internet traffic was dramatically rising</a>. NCSA Mosaic <a href="/enabling/mosaic/awards">won multiple technology awards</a>, including being named 1993 Product of the Year by InfoWorld magazine and 1994 Technology of the Year by IndustryWeek magazine.</p>\n\n<p>The technology was quickly transferred to the private sector, with <a href="http://publicaffairs.illinois.edu/markerstour/north_web_browser.html">Marc Andreessen and several other Mosaic developers</a> launching Netscape and more than 100 companies, including Microsoft, licensing the software through start-up Spyglass, Inc. NCSA <!--<a href="/News/Access/Stories/97Stories/Mosaic.html">-->discontinued support for Mosaic in 1997, shifting its focus to other research and development projects. </p>\n\n<p>The availability of Mosaic and its offspring transformed communication and commerce. Today, calculations put global Web use at over 2 billion individuals, with use growing by more than <a href="http://www.internetworldstats.com/stats.htm">500 percent in the past eleven years</a>. In the United States, \nonline sales now generate more than <a href="http://www.internetretailer.com/trends/sales/">$200 billion in revenue a year</a>.</p>\n\n<p>In 2003, NCSA <a href="/Conferences/MosaicEvent/">celebrated Mosaic\'s tenth birthday</a> by bringing together a panel of computer science luminaries to discuss the impact of Mosaic and its progeny on science, business, and society and what the future might hold for computing, networking, and technology. </p>\n\n<p>The proceedings of the <a href="ftp://ftp.ncsa.uiuc.edu/ncsapubs/IT94/proceedings.zip">Mosaic and the Web International Conference \'94</a> are available for download (20 MB .zip file).</p>\n\t\t<p style="margin-bottom: 100px;">&nbsp;</p>\n\n\t</div> <!-- end #pagetext -->\n</div> <!-- end

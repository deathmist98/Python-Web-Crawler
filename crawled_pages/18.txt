class=\'post-header\'>\n<div class=\'post-header-line-1\'></div>\n</div>\n<div class=\'post-body entry-content\' id=\'post-body-4436562505022949408\' itemprop=\'description articleBody\'>\nNow that <a href="http://googlesystem.blogspot.com/2007/07/google-indexing-many-web-pages-in-real.html">Google indexes pages extremely fast</a> and saves <a href="http://googlesystem.blogspot.com/2007/06/get-fresh-search-results-from-google.html">the date of the first indexing</a>, it would be nice to have more options for <a href="http://googlesystem.blogspot.com/2007/03/restricting-search-to-date-range.html">restricting search results to a date range</a>. Google only provided three options in the advanced search: see all the pages last updated in the past 3, 6 or 12 months and a difficult-to-use operator (daterange).<br /><br />The <a href="http://www.google.com/advanced_search?as_qdr=d">advanced search page</a> has been updated and it shows four more options: find the web pages first indexed in the past day, week, month or in the past 2 months.<br /><br /><img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5104763945575063778" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_ZaGO7GjCqAI/RtfDwyY5IOI/AAAAAAAAEhw/t1otGWnJ-Ys/s640/advanced-date-search.png" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center;" /><br />If you remove all the uninteresting parameters from the search URL, you\'ll find that <span style="font-weight:bold;">as_qdr</span> is responsible for date restrictions. For example, here\'s how to restrict a search for [China] to pages first seen by Google\'s crawler in the past 24 hours:<br /><br /><a href="http://www.google.com/search?q=china&as_qdr=d">http://www.google.com/search?q=china&as_qdr=d</a><br /><br />Note that you\'ll only find new web pages and not pages that were updated in the past 24 hours. That means you won\'t find homepages from popular sites or other frequently-updated pages. If the date range is small, you\'ll mostly find news and blog posts.<br /><br />The nice thing is that you can change the value of <span style="font-weight:bold;">as_qdr</span> to custom intervals. Here are all the possible values of the <span style="font-weight:bold;">as_qdr</span> parameter:<br /><br />d[number] - past number of days (e.g.: d10)<br />w[number] - past number of weeks<br />y[number] - past number of years<br /><br />For example, <a href="http://www.google.com/search?q=china&as_qdr=d10">http://www.google.com/search?q=china&as_qdr=d10</a> lets you search for pages that contain "China" and were created in the past 10 days.<br /><br /><img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5104763945575063778" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_ZaGO7GjCqAI/RtfHJCY5IPI/AAAAAAAAEh8/EhR8dWKxeQ8/s640/search-10-days.png" style="margin: 0px auto 10px; display: block; text-align: center;" /><br />A finer control (hours) and an option to sort the results by date would make this feature almost perfect.\n<div style=\'clear: both;\'></div>\n</div>\n<div class=\'post-ad\'>\n<script type=\'text/javascript\'>\n
